Solar Power Explained

Solar power is a renewable energy source derived from the sun's radiation. It is harnessed using various technologies such as solar panels, photovoltaic cells, and solar thermal systems. Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity, while solar thermal systems use sunlight to generate heat for applications like water heating or electricity generation through steam turbines. Solar power is considered environmentally friendly, as it produces no greenhouse gas emissions during electricity generation and contributes to reducing reliance on fossil fuels. It has the potential to play a significant role in sustainable energy solutions globally.
